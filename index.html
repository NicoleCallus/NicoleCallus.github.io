High key and Low key Lighting in Photography

Definition and Aspects

High key Lighting

High key lighting is used to produce a brightly lit frame with soft lighting, minimal shadows and less contrast between the darker tones and the brighter areas. Due to he bright lighting used the lighting ratio in the scene is reduced. In photography, film and television, it uses minimal shadows with most of the frame composed of highlights. The bright lighting creates a cheerful, light-hearted and positive mood. Because of this high key lighting is used in sitcoms, comedies, marketing commercials and dramas to impart a positive message and a joyful tone. It is effective in the case of video interviews and training videos since it implies truth and sincerity. In the high key lighting film scene, there are no distractions from the lighting on the mood of the film.

Low key Lighting

Low key lighting is achieved by using hard sources or key lights with minimal to no fill light. This in turn creates minimal highlights and midtones. In photography, film and television, it accentuates shadows, high contrast between the light and the dark tones. Low key lighting directs the viewer's attention towards either the darker emotional part of the storyline or the serious subject matter. Because of this low key lighting is used in thrillers, dramas and horror films to create a serious, dark and dramatic mood. It is used in crime dramas to enhance the mysteriousness of a scene or film. In fact in the low key lighting scene, the audience lack of vision will evoke an atmosphere of fear and worry and so is suitable in films filled with tension, intense section or eerie feeling.

Summary

High key lighting

  • Minimal shadows
  • Soft lighting
  • Bright frame composed of highlights
  • Low contrast
  • Cheerful and positive mood

Low key lighting

  • Harsh shadows
  • Deep dark tones
  • Minimal highlights and midtones
  • High contrast
  • Dramatic and mysterious mood

Lighting set up

High key lighting

  • The 3-point lighting technique is the main set up used.
  • The lighting is placed at a 30 degree angle from the camera to create three-dimensionality.
  • The lighting ratio is 1:1 since the fill light projects the same level of brightness as the key light.
  • Large soft boxes and diffusers are used to create soft and diffused lighting.
  • Subjects are placed behind a lighter background to reflect the ambient light.

Low key lighting

  • The ambient light is limited and the key light is controlled.
  • The fill light is the main source of light that is used to create three-dimensionality.
  • The light source is controlled so as to create harsh shadows and darker tones.
  • The lighting ratio is 8:1 since the key light outpowers the fill light.
  • Subjects are placed behind a dark background to reduce the reflection of the ambient light.
